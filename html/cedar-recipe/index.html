Cheesecake with blueberries

Ingredients:

  • Blueberry puree
    • 2 cups blueberries
    • 2 tbsp granulated sugar
    • 2 tsp lemon juice
  • Crust
    • 9 graham crackers, finely crushed (about 1 ¼ cups)
    • 6 tbsp melted butter
    • ¼ cup granulated sugar
  • Cheesecake
    • 4 x 8oz blocks cream cheese, softened
    • 1 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 large eggs
    • 1 tsp vanilla extract
    • ¼ cup sour cream
    • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
    • ¼ tsp kosher salt
    • 1 cup blueberry puree
  • Topping (optional)
    • Whipped cream
    • Blueberries

Method:

  1. Blend blueberries in a small food processor until no large chunks remain.
  2. In a small saucepan over medium heat, add blueberry puree, sugar, and lemon juice. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and let simmer until slightly reduced, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes. Let the blueberry puree cool to room temperature.
  3. Preheat oven to 325° f. Make the crust by mixing together graham cracker crumbs, melted butter, and sugar, until mixture resembles wet sand. Press into bottom and up sides (slightly) of an 8” or 9” springform pan.
  4. Make the cheesecake in a large bowl using a hand mixer (or a stand mixer). Beat cream cheese and sugar until no lumps remain, and then add eggs, one at a time. Stir in vanilla and sour cream. Add flour and salt and beat until just combined. Fold in the blueberry puree.
  5. Pour the cheesecake mixture over the crust. Wrap bottom of pan in aluminium foil and place in a large roasting pan. Pour in enough boiling water to come halfway up the cake pan.
  6. Bake until centre of cheesecake only slightly jiggles, about 1 hour 30 minutes. Turn off heat, prop open oven door and let the cheesecake cool in oven for about 1 hour.
  7. Remove foil and refrigerate cheesecake until completely chilled, at least 5 hours and up to overnight.
  8. When ready to serve, top cheesecake with whipped cream and blueberries.
    Note: Makes one tall, 9” cheesecake or one smaller cheesecake (6-7”) plus 6 cupcake size cheesecakes.
